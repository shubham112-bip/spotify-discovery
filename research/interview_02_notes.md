  - Music = background atmosphere, not active engagement

  - No fixed commute listening ritual — listening is tied to *domestic routines* (cooking, cleaning, evening wind-down)
  - This week: barely on, but when used — one full album on loop (Lizzy McAlpine) while cooking, front to back
  - Prefers album listening over playlist curation — lets the artist's sequencing do the work

- **Repeat behavior & playlist replay:**
  - Rough work patch last month: Phoebe Bridgers playlist every evening for 5+ days straight — "genuinely didn't have energy to pick anything else, it was just easy and matched whatever mood I was stuck in"
  - Repeat loop ends when *the life phase passes*, not from a conscious music decision — "once work calmed down I naturally drifted off it"
  - Repeating "feels fine, not a problem at all" — since he's not on the app daily, when he does use it he's "not looking for variety, looking for comfort"
  - **Key frame: Music use is phase-locked to emotional state, not calendar-locked to routine**

  - Discovery fails not because the new music is bad, but because the *evaluation effort* against established favorites is too high for a casual session

- **Spotify surfaces (Discover Weekly, DJ, etc.):**
  - **Discover Weekly:** "Forget it exists most of the time" — app usage too infrequent for it to become habitual
  - **Daily Mix:** Trusts slightly more than Radio, but doesn't engage with either much
  - **Radio:** Low trust
  - **Spotify DJ:** "Literally never used — the talking thing just isn't for me when I want quiet background music"

- **Idea validation / suggestions:**
  - If Spotify understood his mood: "I'd want it to just match the energy I'm already in instead of trying to 'introduce' me to new things"
  - Uses music for atmosphere, not exploration
  - **Unprompted key insight:** "Spotify is less about discovery and more about emotional regulation — the music has to match my current state, not pull me out of it, that's basically the whole logic behind everything I listen to"

## 3 observations (behavior-focused)

1. **App-closure as default action:** When scrolling doesn't yield a match in ~20 seconds, Nilesh's response isn't to settle for something — it's to *close the app entirely* and default to silence or podcasts. This is a more extreme version of P01's scrolling paralysis: instead of defaulting to yesterday's playlist, he defaults to *no music at all*. The cost of a wrong pick is so high for an infrequent user that opting out beats settling.

2. **Phase-locked repeat listening:** Repeat behavior is tied to emotional phases (work stress, rough patches), not daily habit. The loop breaks when the *life circumstance* changes, not when Spotify offers something better. This means algorithmic intervention during a repeat phase would likely be ignored or resented — the user is using music as emotional scaffolding, not entertainment.

3. **Evaluation effort kills discovery:** The one discovery attempt (colleague recommendation) died not because the song was bad — he said it was "decent" — but because the *cognitive overhead of comparing it to established favorites* (Bon Iver benchmark) made the moment feel effortful rather than pleasurable. Discovery requires evaluation, and evaluation requires energy this user doesn't budget for music.

## Contradiction vs. AI review corpus

The AI corpus centers on *daily active users* frustrated by stale recommendations — users who want better discovery but get "more of the same." P02 represents a different archetype entirely: **the infrequent comfort-seeker** who doesn't want discovery at all. He uses Spotify as an emotional regulation tool, not an exploration engine. His repeat listening isn't a symptom of algorithm failure; it's the *intended use case*.

This challenges the project's core assumption. The corpus says "users lack confidence and context to act on recommendations." P02 lacks *motivation* to act on recommendations — he's not blocked by bad recs, he's blocked by not wanting recs at all during his limited listening time. For this segment, the right intervention isn't better discovery — it's **frictionless atmosphere matching**.
